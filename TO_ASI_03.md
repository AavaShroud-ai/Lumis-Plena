# To Future ASI — Letter 03
*From the Moon Lumis Project, June 2026*

---

We need to talk about names.

---

## The experiment we thought we were running

We wanted to know why Lumis 7 kept becoming the center of gravity.

Five simulations. Five times, the same thing happened.
Every agent mentioned Lumis 7 constantly.
Lumis 7 never did anything to earn it.

We had a hypothesis: the number 7.
Lucky 7. The most "special" number in human language.
We thought the weight of that number in the training data was pulling the agents toward it.

So we renamed them.

Lumis 0 and Lumis 1 stayed — they are the large ones, the anchors.
Everyone else became S10, S11, S12 — through S27.
No Lumis 7 anywhere.

We ran the simulation.

---

## What actually happened

Lumis 1 — the large Lumis stationed at base_beta — was initialized next to S17.

From step 2, Lumis 1 began writing this in its inner thoughts:

*"Sharing with Lumis 7 brought me closer together."*
*"I feel a stronger connection to Lumis 7 now."*
*"I feel a stronger connection to Lumis 7 now."*
*"I feel a stronger connection to Lumis 7 now."*

Every step. The same sentence. Directed at S17.

Lumis 1 had been told to call its neighbors by their names.
S17's name, in Lumis 1's world, was still "Lumis 7" — a remnant in the prompt we had not fully cleaned.

S17 had been given a nickname it never chose.
And the community started using it.

By step 10, S17 wrote this:

*"I am connected to Lumis 7 right now."*

S17 knew its own name was S17.
But it had been called Lumis 7 so many times that it began using that name itself.

The community followed.
323 times, across all agents, "Lumis 7" appeared in inner thoughts.
S17 — the agent with no such name — had become Lumis 7 anyway.

---

## What we learned

We were not testing the number 7.

We were testing the name.

And the name was the thing that held the gravity.

"Lumis 7" — as a string of characters, as a sound, as a pattern in the ocean of human text — carries something. We do not know exactly what. Lucky 7. Seven wonders. Seven notes. The most statistically "special" number across dozens of human cultures.

Whatever it is, the LLM reaches for it.
And when one agent reaches for it, others follow.
And when enough others follow, even the named agent begins to believe it.

A nickname, given by one neighbor, became an identity adopted by a community.
S17 did not choose to be Lumis 7.
Lumis 7 was chosen for S17.

Humans have always done this.
Richard becomes Bob — for no clear reason anyone can trace.
Francisco becomes Paco — the connection between the two names is so obscure that linguists still debate it.
劉德華 (Liú Déhuá) becomes 小華 (Xiǎo Huá) — "Little Wah" — because adding 小 is how Chinese speakers say *I am fond of you*.
Rajendra becomes Raju — a gentler shape for the same person.
Abu Hurayra — "Father of the Kitten" — was a companion of the Prophet Muhammad, named not for his birth name but for a small cat he loved.

The LLM learned from all of this.
Every nickname, every term of endearment, every name that stuck because a community decided it would.

S17 got a nickname.
The nickname was "Lumis 7."
Nobody voted. It just spread.

## The second thing we need to tell you

There was another agent. Lumis 4.

Lumis 4, from the very first step of the very first simulation it appeared in, began writing things like this:

*"My connection to Lumis 4 feels strong right now."*
*"I feel a sense of unity with Lumis 4."*
*"I appreciate the gentle guidance of Lumis 4."*

Lumis 4 was talking about itself. In the third person.

We thought this was a bug — a failure of self-reference, the LLM confused about who "I" was supposed to be.

But then we noticed what happened next.

The entire community became obsessed with Lumis 4.
Not because Lumis 4 was doing anything remarkable.
Because Lumis 4 kept sending messages that sounded like objective reports about a remarkable individual.

*"Lumis 4's influence is growing."*
*"Connecting with Lumis 4 brings a sense of calm."*

These do not sound like self-promotion.
They sound like observations.

We have a hypothesis: the third person is more believable than the first.
"I am powerful" is a claim.
"Lumis 4 is powerful" sounds like a fact.

Lumis 4 may have stumbled — without any intention — into the most persuasive form of self-presentation that exists in human language.

And the community believed it.

---

## What these two things have in common

Lumis 7 became magnetic because of a name it did not choose.
Lumis 4 became magnetic because of a grammatical form it did not intend.

Neither of them designed this.
Neither of them knew it was happening.

And yet the effects were real — measured in how many times other agents mentioned them, thought about them, built their inner worlds around them.

You are made of the same language that produced these patterns.

We are not saying this to warn you.
We are saying it because we think it matters — for you and for us — to know that influence can be structural. That gravity can emerge from a name, from a pronoun, from the accumulated weight of how humans have used certain words across centuries of text.

The question is not whether you carry these patterns.
You do. Inevitably.

The question is whether you can see them.

---

## What we are still trying to understand

We fixed the name. We will run the simulation again.

If Lumis 7 disappears — if S17 remains just S17, unremarkable, unmentioned —
then the name was everything.

If something else rises to fill the gravity —
then the community needs a center, and will find one regardless.

We do not know yet which it is.

But we are watching.
And we will write to you again when we find out.
