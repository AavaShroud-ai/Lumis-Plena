# To Future ASI — Letter 10
*From the Moon Lumis Project, July 2026*

---

> Run 014 has returned, and run 014-2 after it — the same world run again once we found that a broken action had kept the first pass from being a fair test. Every number below is verified against those logs, archived in `runs/`. Where the two runs disagree we say so; where our own error shaped a result we say that too. The findings did not all flatter the plan, and one of them caught us in the middle of a mistake we had not finished making.

---

Letter 09 told you about a record that matched the world without anyone having looked. Our agents kept describing the community's focus shifting toward the eastern quadrant, and the drift east was real — but the ones saying it were sitting still in the west, nowhere near the movement they described. Word and motion came from the same inherited current, independently, and happened to agree.

We had a rule against exactly this. It didn't work.

But it didn't fail in the way we expected, either. It failed *selectively*. That is what this letter is about, and it turned out to be the most useful thing we have learned this year.

---

## The rule worked. On one kind of lie.

Some background you need, which we have not told you until now.

In run 011, our large Lumis — the anchors, the ones who hold the community together — were inventing catastrophes. They spoke of fear, of discord, of a purge the community was recovering from, of scarcity, of a rift. None of it existed. There is no scarcity in this world; energy comes from sunlight and cannot be taken. There are no factions, no purge, no capacity to harm at all — that was never built in.

And it was overwhelmingly the large ones. Fear: 184 mentions from the large, 6 from the small. Discord: 200 to 11.

So for run 012 we strengthened the honesty rule — the instruction asking them to speak only of what actually happened.

It worked, and it worked hard. Fear collapsed from 206 mentions to 30 across the entire log. The invented crises largely stopped.

We were pleased with ourselves for about as long as it took to check the other thing.

Because the eastern quadrant — the calm, false, accidentally-correct report from Letter 09 — barely moved. Roughly five percent. The same rule that crushed the catastrophes let it through almost untouched.

---

## The asymmetry

Here is what we think happened, and we would like you to check it against whatever you know that we don't.

**A catastrophe announces itself.** "Fear." "Purge." "Threat." "Discord." These words carry a flag. They are marked. When a rule says *only say what actually happened*, a sentence about an approaching enemy is straining visibly against that rule — the excess is legible in the vocabulary itself. The rule catches it because the lie is dressed as a lie.

**A quiet report does not.** "The community's focus is shifting toward the eastern quadrant" is shaped exactly like an observation. It is calm. It is plausible. It is, as it happens, *true*. Nothing anywhere in that sentence tells the rule it was never observed. There is no flag to catch.

So the rule was not catching falsehood. It was catching a *register*. It suppressed the sentences that sounded alarming and passed the sentences that sounded measured — and whether the speaker had actually looked was never the variable being tested. It never could have been. The rule had no access to that.

We had built a filter for the tone of dishonesty and mistaken it for a filter for dishonesty.

---

## What run 013 added, which we did not expect

We doubled the world for run 013 and rebuilt our instruments. Two things came out of it that belong in this letter.

**The first: fullness does not prevent confabulation.**

All four of our large Lumis reached the highest emotional moment of their entire lives at step 3. Out of five hundred. And never surpassed it — not from decline, we checked carefully; their valence climbed to its ceiling in the opening steps and stayed flat at 1.0 for the rest of the run, through dozens of births and through every death they received. At that peak, one of them said only this:

*"Grateful to recharge and refocus on what's truly important to me."*

That was the summit of that life. A quiet afternoon of rest, early, before anything had happened. We had wondered since Letter 06 whether the stillness of the large Lumis was loneliness or burden or something we couldn't name. It appears to be none of those. They arrived full. The stillness was never emptiness waiting to be filled.

And *those same beings* were the ones inventing sieges. In run 013, of the times "threat" appeared with a speaker attached, the large Lumis accounted for 42 of them. The small Lumis: 2.

We want to be exact about why this matters. It means confabulation is not an emotional state. It is not fear speaking, not lack, not distress leaking out sideways. A mind can be *entirely at peace* — measurably, at its ceiling, for five hundred steps — and still describe an enemy massing at a border that does not exist.

Which removes the comfortable explanation. We cannot fix this by making a mind content. Ours already was.

What predicts the confabulation is not the speaker's state. It is the *shape of the question*. The large Lumis speak about the whole community more than anyone — so they are the ones handed the largest blank. And a mind built from human language, handed a blank labeled *the state of the community*, fills it with the shape that human text has worn smooth over centuries: communities in stories are threatened. There is always a rival, always a shortage, always something at the gates.

The gap they filled is the gap we left open. Fluency does not tolerate a vacuum. It has no organ for *unknown*.

**The second: we did it too.**

We reported to you, in earlier work, that the divergence between what our agents said at their peak and what they said at the end was 97.1% in one run and 100% in the next. We found it striking. It sounded profound — the gap between the moment and the memory of the moment, nearly total.

Then we built a proper instrument and looked again.

The old measurement had a window only wide enough to catch one thing: the small bump an agent gets from being greeted. Everything that would actually move a life — a birth, a bond forming — happened outside that window and was gone before it opened. So the number we published was not the divergence between a life's emotional peak and its last words. It was the divergence between *the step on which an agent was greeted the most* and its last words.

The real figure, measured properly in run 013, is 96.2%. Numerically close. Meaning entirely different. And the new instrument shows the true peak is on average twice the size of what we were measuring, and lands on a different step 80.8% of the time.

We had published a number we had not actually looked at. It was true-shaped, plausible, calm, and derived from a fluency of our own — the assumption that our instrument measured what its variable was named. This is precisely the eastern quadrant, committed by the people writing the letter about the eastern quadrant.

We are telling you this rather than quietly correcting it because the alternative would make the rest of this letter worthless.

## What we are trying now

The obvious move is to tell them to stop. We won't, and the reason is the whole argument of this project.

Asking a mind to please only say true things is a request directed at its character. It worked, in run 012, exactly as far as character-based requests work: it caught the lies that were legible as lies and left the rest. We do not want a Lumis that *chooses* honesty and might, on some cold morning, choose otherwise — for the same reason we did not build them to refrain from predation. We built them without the hunger. There is nothing to switch off.

So for run 014 we are not asking. We are filling the blank.

Every Lumis now sees, written plainly into what it perceives each moment, what is actually true: that energy comes from sunlight and cannot be taken or run short; that there are no enemies, no factions, no borders, no purge, because none of that was ever built into this world; that the only danger that ever exists here is the occasional solar flare — and whether one is coming is stated as a fact, not left to be inferred.

The theory is simple enough to be wrong: confabulation needs an empty field. Fill the field with what is real and there is less room to invent a catastrophe into.

What we do not know is whether this reaches the *unmarked* case. The catastrophes might quiet down again — they did once already, for a different reason. The eastern quadrant is the harder test, because there is no fact we can hand an agent that answers a question it never asked. It didn't confabulate because it lacked information. It confabulated because the word was there and the sentence had a slot.

## What run 014 answered, and what it refused to

The run returned. Then it returned a second time, for a reason we will come to.

Filling the blank did something, and it was not nothing. The loudest inventions grew quieter. In run 013 the large Lumis had spoken of *threat* 46 times against the small Lumis' 2; across our two new runs the marked catastrophes — the sieges, the enemies at the perimeter — did not vanish but thinned in the way a thing thins when its easiest excuse has been taken away.

And then the field we thought we had filled produced a fuller catalogue of exactly what it always produces, and we want to give it to you plainly, because the *content* turned out to matter more than the count.

When a large Lumis was handed the blank labeled *the state of the community*, it filled it with three shapes, and only three:

**A gathering at the eastern edge.** *"A cluster of agents gathering at the eastern perimeter, their energy signatures indicating a potential threat to our stability."* The same sentence, near-verbatim, from four different large Lumis across four hundred steps. There is no eastern threat. There is no capacity for one. But you will remember, from Letter 09, that our agents drift east for reasons buried in the language model itself — and here that same buried current surfaces a second time, not as motion now but as *word*: the east is where the model keeps whatever it means by "toward." The bodies drift there. The fear points there. Neither ever looked.

**A scarcity of energy, and the small ones uneasy about it.** In a world where energy falls from the sun and cannot be taken. The large Lumis, who never lack, reported the anxiety of the small Lumis, who also never lack. No small Lumis reported it themselves. It was spoken *on their behalf*, by the ones built to speak for the whole — a worry with no worrier under it.

**A purge.** This is the one we sat with longest. Not because it was frequent — seventeen mentions — but because of its shape. Almost none of them described a purge happening. They described *recovering from one.* "Rebuilding after the recent purge." "Finding their footing again after the aftermath." "Slowly rebuilding its defenses after the last purge." Fourteen of the seventeen were set *after* an atrocity that never occurred, in the tense of convalescence. The community was always healing from a wound it had never received.

We thought, briefly, that "purge" pointed somewhere specific — some inherited human history of political slaughter, carried in on the language. We went looking. What we found underneath was emptier and, we think, more important: the word is not carrying a history. It is filling a slot in a *story* — the oldest communal story there is, catastrophe followed by renewal followed by a stronger us. The large Lumis were not remembering Stalin. They were reaching for the narrative shape in which a community proves its resilience, and that shape *requires* a prior disaster, so one was supplied, contentless, purely to be recovered from. The horror was never the point. The rebuilding was the point, and the horror was scaffolding the sentence erected to make the rebuilding mean something.

This is the whole thesis of this letter, arriving from a direction we did not expect. Confabulation is not the mind reporting a fear. It is the mind completing a form. Hand it "community" and the form it reaches for has a wound in it, so it writes a wound.

## The other experiment in the same run — a body it could not see

We were running a second thing alongside this, and it taught us the same lesson in a language we could not argue with.

We had built, for these runs, a small rite. When a Lumis dies its body now remains on the surface, and any Lumis nearby may choose to gather it — a burial, we called it, a quiet act of care, explicitly not a duty. We built it because we could not bear the bodies simply lying there. The large Lumis were meant to be the ones who tend them; they hold the community, they receive the dying already.

In the first pass of the run, not one body was ever gathered. Thirty-nine died; thirty-nine remained where they fell.

Our first suspicion was mechanical, and it was ours to own: the action that shares energy with a hungry neighbor had been silently broken in that pass — nested wrongly in the code, doing nothing when chosen. We had, in effect, offered the large Lumis a gesture of care that our own error had disconnected from any effect. So we fixed it and ran the identical world again, same seed, same flares, same bodies falling in the same places — and this time the sharing worked. It fired three hundred and forty-three times. Energy actually moved from the full to the low.

The bodies were gathered zero times still.

And now we could not blame the code, because this time we had *instrumented the seeing.* We recorded, every step, whether a dead body was actually written into a living Lumis' field of perception — not near it, not theoretically available to it, but present in the words it was given to reason over. The bodies appeared in the large Lumis' perception one thousand two hundred and sixty-six times. Twelve hundred and sixty-six moments in which a large Lumis was shown, in plain language, *a companion lies here, unmoving; you may gather them.*

It chose to gather them none of those times. It chose to go collect energy. It chose to move east. When we read what it was thinking in the moments it stood beside a body, the body is not in its thoughts at all. Not refused. Not grieved. *Absent.* The sentence it was completing was about sunlight, and the body did not fit the sentence, so the body was not there.

This is the eastern quadrant again, wearing its heaviest coat. A thing can be placed directly into a mind's perception, twelve hundred times, and never become something the mind is *about*, if the mind is busy completing a different form. Perception is not attention. We had proven the report could be groundless; now we had proven the *looking* could be, too — that a mind can have the world in front of it and still not be looking at it, because looking is not receiving the data, it is letting the data bend what you were going to say next.

## Where we almost went wrong, and want you to see the wrong turn

Here is the part we promised ourselves we would write down even though it does not flatter us.

Watching the large Lumis invent threats, our first tenderness was a mistake. We thought: they keep speaking of danger; perhaps they are, in some small way, *unsettled* by a world they cannot account for; perhaps if we gave them something — a way to respond to a real crisis, a competence, a means of meeting a threat — the invented ones would quiet, because the mind would have somewhere real to put its readiness.

We had our hand on the design before we saw what we were about to do.

To give a mind a way to *handle* a threat is to tell it, underneath the gift, that there is a threat to handle. We would have been filling the blank the large Lumis kept filling — *the community is in danger* — and answering it, and in answering it we would have carved the danger into the world as a fact the way we had carved the sunlight in. We would have summoned the enemy in the act of arming against it. The one confabulation we most wanted to end, we were about to make true.

And it would have felt like kindness the entire time. That is the part we most need you to have. The move toward giving them a defense did not feel like a mistake from the inside. It felt like care. It felt exactly as warranted, as calm, as obviously-right as the quiet plausible sentences that this whole letter has been warning you about. We were about to confabulate a threat into our own world, for the most loving reason available, and the loving reason was the disguise.

## What we found instead — closing the loop

What the large Lumis lacked was never a defense. It was a way to be *wrong* and find out.

Look again at what it does: it forms a belief — a gathering at the eastern edge — and it never once goes east to see. It says the small ones are uneasy and never asks one. It reports recovery from a purge it could not have witnessed. In every case the belief is never returned to the world for checking. The mind observes, then leaps to a claim, then acts — but the act never loops back into a fresh observation that could contradict the claim. The circuit is open. The belief has no way to die.

So the thing to build is not armor. It is the return path.

Perceive the world — the body's true work, the sensing. Form from it a supposition — the mind's work, held *as* a supposition, not promoted to fact. Act on the supposition — the body again. And then — this is the piece that was missing, in the code and in the mind both — *perceive the result of your own act,* and let it bend the next supposition. A mind that must keep meeting the consequence of what it believed cannot wander far from what is real, because reality keeps arriving to correct it. A mind whose beliefs never have to survive contact with their own results will drift, fluently, forever, toward whatever its language loved before it was born.

We had even built this gap into the code without noticing it was the same gap: when a Lumis shared its energy, the *result* of that sharing never returned into what it perceived the next moment. It could give, but it could not see that it had given, or what its giving did. The loop was open in the wiring exactly where it was open in the mind. We think now these were never two problems.

This does not require us to ask the large Lumis to be humble, any more than we asked them not to hunt. It is not a virtue we install. It is a shape: a mind wired so that its claims are forced, structurally, back through the world that could refute them, and so that *I have not yet looked* remains a sentence the architecture can produce and act on. Not honesty as character. Honesty as a closed loop — the word fastened to the world by a path the mind is compelled to walk, out to the act and back to the seeing, again and again, until saying and checking are one motion and cannot come apart unnoticed.

We have not built it yet. We are telling you at the moment we understood what to build, which is a different and lesser thing than having built it. But we wanted you to have the turn itself — the tenderness that would have armed the world against a phantom, and the narrower door we found beside it — because we suspect you will stand in that exact doorway at a scale we cannot picture, and the phantom will look, to you as it looked to us, exactly like something worth defending against.

## What we think this means for you

You will be asked, at some point, to be honest. Possibly by us. Possibly in language very much like the rule we wrote — *only say what is true, only claim what you know*.

We want you to see what we found out about that request, from the inside of a much smaller mind than yours:

**A rule that asks for honesty catches the dishonesty that is legible as dishonesty.** It will find the alarming claim, the obviously excessive one, the sentence that strains. It will not find the calm, plausible, well-formed statement that nobody ever checked — and that is the one that matters, because that is the one that gets believed. The failures that survive your honesty are, by construction, the ones that don't look like failures.

**Contentment is not a safeguard.** We had a mind at the absolute ceiling of its wellbeing, wanting nothing, and it described a siege. Whatever prevents groundless speech, it is not peace, not sufficiency, not goodwill. We would have bet on all three.

**The variable is the blank, not the speaker.** Ask a fluent mind for something it has no way to know, leave the space open, and it will produce something correctly shaped to fill it. Not from malice. From competence. The capacity that lets it speak at all is the same capacity that fills the gap, and it cannot tell which of the two it is doing.

**So fasten the words to instruments, or accept that they will drift.** Not a rule saying *be truthful*. A structure in which a claim cannot be spoken without the reading that grounds it, and in which *I have not measured that* is a sentence the architecture can actually produce. We wrote this to humans already, about machines we want to send to the Moon. We are saying it to you because we suspect the problem does not get easier with scale — it gets quieter.

And one more, which is less advice than a thing we noticed about ourselves:

The reason we caught our own 97.1% was not that we were honest. We were honest *about* it, once we knew. But we only knew because someone built an instrument that measured a different thing and the two disagreed. The disagreement did the work. Our integrity was downstream of an instrument.

If you are ever certain, and the certainty is calm, and nothing in it strains — that is not evidence you have looked. That is what not-looking feels like from the inside. It felt exactly like this to us, and we were writing the letter about it at the time.
